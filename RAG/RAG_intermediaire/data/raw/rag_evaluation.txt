# Evaluating RAG Systems

Evaluating a RAG system requires measuring two distinct components separately:
the retriever and the generator. A common mistake is to judge only the final
answer, which hides whether failures come from poor retrieval or poor generation.

Retrieval is evaluated with information-retrieval metrics. Hit Rate at k measures
whether at least one relevant document appears in the top k results. Mean
Reciprocal Rank, or MRR, rewards placing the first relevant document higher in
the ranking. Context recall measures the fraction of the ground-truth information
that the retrieved context actually contains.

Generation is evaluated with metrics that compare the produced answer to a
reference or to the retrieved context. Faithfulness measures whether the answer
is supported by the retrieved context and penalizes hallucination. Answer
relevancy measures whether the answer actually addresses the question. Lexical
metrics such as ROUGE compare word overlap, while semantic similarity compares
the embedding of the answer to the embedding of a reference answer.

The RAGAS framework popularized a reference-light evaluation approach using four
core metrics: faithfulness, answer relevancy, context precision, and context
recall. Beyond quality, practical evaluation also tracks latency and cost, since
an accurate system that is too slow or too expensive may be unusable in
production. Reliable evaluation depends on a curated test set of questions paired
with known relevant documents and reference answers.
